Masjid al-Aqsa The mosque of Prophets Masjid al-Aqsa is the mosque that holds significance for most of the prophets sent to Earth by Allah. the first time it was built by Hazrat Adam (AS) as a place of worship. Some people hold the opinion that it was first built by Hazrat Ibrahim (AS). Bayt-ul-Maqdis was rebuilt and renovated many times. Masjid al-Aqsa was a place of worship even during the times Hazrat Ibrahim’s sons Hazrat Ismail (AS) and Hazrat Ishaaq (AS). His grandson Hazrat Yaqub (AS) and his son Hazrat Yusuf (AS) also witnessed the glory of Masjid al-Aqsa as a reverent place of worship. Hazrat Dawood (AS) conquered Palestine and ruled over Jerusalem. Masjid al-Aqsa was reconstructed by his son Prophet Suleiman (AS). The reverence of Bayt-ul-Maqdis was such that the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) used to talk about the mosque quite often to his companions. Hundreds of Allah’s prophets and messengers are buried at this place. Even many of companions are laid to rest here.
